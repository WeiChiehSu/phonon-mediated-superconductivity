$$
由於計算\lambda _{q}和\alpha ^{2}F(\omega )需要在Fermi-surface進行雙重\delta積分計算電子態密度,但電腦無法計算\delta方程,為了處理\delta方程,需要使用broadening(展寬)方法進行\delta方程近似:
$$

$$
Since{}the{}calculation{}of{}\lambda_{q}{}and{}\alpha^{2}F(\omega){}requires{}performing{}double{}\delta-function{}integrations{}over{}the{}electronic{}states{}on{}the{}Fermi{}surface,{}and{}computers{}cannot{}directly{}evaluate{}\delta{}functions,{}a{}broadening{}method{}is{}used{}to{}approximate{}the{}\delta{}functions.
$$

$$
broadening:I=\int_{BZ}^{}dq \int_{BZ}^{}d\epsilon f(k,q)\delta (\epsilon _{n,K}-\epsilon _{F})\delta (\epsilon _{n^{'} ,k^{'}}-\epsilon _{F})
$$

$$
QE中使用broadening法時,先將k點網格平均切割成的好幾個區間,每個區間中用一組'類似,模糊'的光滑連率函數去近似成尖銳地\delta方程(\sigma=degauss),去計算系統的電子佔據數,最終得到電子態密度.
$$

$$
When{}using{}the{}broadening{}method{}in{}Quantum{}ESPRESSO{}(QE),{}the{}k-point{}grid{}is{}first{}divided{}into{}many{}small{}intervals.{}Within{}each{}interval,{}a{}smooth,{}“smeared”{}function{}is{}used{}to{}approximate{}the{}sharp{}\delta-function{}(with{}width{}\sigma=degauss).{}This{}allows{}the{}electronic{}occupations{}to{}be{}calculated,{}from{}which{}the{}electronic{}density{}of{}states{}is{}ultimately{}obtained.
$$

$$
將broadening引入後,雙重\delta積分近似為:
$$

$$
After{}introducing{}broadening,{}the{}double{}\delta-function{}integral{}can{}be{}approximated{}as:
$$

$$
I\simeq \frac{\Omega _{BZ}^{2} }{N_{K} N_{q}} \sum_{k}^{} \sum_{q}^{}  \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi \sigma } }e^{-\frac{(\epsilon _{k}-\epsilon _{F})^{2}   }{\sigma ^{2} } } \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi \sigma } }e^{-\frac{(\epsilon _{k^{'}}-\epsilon _{F})^{2}   }{\sigma ^{2} } }   
$$

Eliashbreg譜函數可近似為:

The Eliashberg spectral function can be approximated as:

$$
\alpha ^{2}F(\omega )\simeq \frac{1 }{2N_{K} N_{q}N(\varepsilon _{F})\omega _{q}} \sum_{nn^{'}}^{} \sum_{k}^{} \sum_{q}^{}\left |  g_{nn^{'}}(k,q) ^{2} \right | \delta (\omega -\omega _{q})  \frac{1}{2\pi \sigma  }e^{-\frac{(\epsilon _{k}-\epsilon _{F})^{2}   }{\sigma ^{2} } } e^{-\frac{(\epsilon _{k^{'}}-\epsilon _{F})^{2}   }{\sigma ^{2} } } 
$$

broadening法可以用費米-狄拉克分布去進行理解:

The broadening method can be understood in terms of the Fermi–Dirac distribution:

![圖片描述](https://github.com/WeiChiehSu/phonon-mediated-superconductivity/blob/main/introduction%20to%20The%20theory%20of%20First%20principle/degauss.png)

$$
\sigma(degauss)便是控制費米-狄拉克分布參數平緩或陡峭的參數:\sigma值越小,費米-狄拉克分布越陡峭;,\sigma值越大,費米-狄拉克分布越平緩
$$

$$
\sigma{}(degauss){}is{}the{}parameter{}that{}controls{}how{}sharp{}or{}smooth{}the{}Fermi–Dirac{}distribution{}is:{}the{}smaller{}the{}value{}of{}\sigma,{}the{}steeper{}the{}Fermi–Dirac{}distribution;{}the{}larger{}the{}value{}of{}\sigma,{}the{}smoother{}the{}Fermi–Dirac{}distribution.
$$

k點網格平均切割成的區間,可以當成k(0.8-1.2)的範圍:k點網格切點值越疏,範圍越大;k點網格切點值越密,範圍越小

The intervals obtained by dividing the k-point grid can be regarded as ranges such as k(0.8–1.2): when the k-point grid is sparse, the range is larger; when the k-point grid is dense, the range is smaller.

$$
從費米-狄拉克分布的圖可以注意到:k點網格切點密度需要和\sigma值匹配!倘若k點網格切點密度很高,\sigma很低,會造成低估電子態密度;倘若k點網格切點密度很小,\sigma很高,會造成嚴重高估電子態密度和導致數值計算補償,讓計算出的電子佔據數出現負值!
$$

$$
From{}the{}Fermi–Dirac{}distribution,{}it{}can{}be{}seen{}that{}the{}k-point{}grid{}density{}must{}be{}properly{}matched{}with{}the{}value{}of{}\sigma.{}If{}the{}k-point{}grid{}density{}is{}very{}high{}while{}\sigma{}is{}very{}small,{}the{}electronic{}density{}of{}states{}will{}be{}underestimated.{}Conversely,{}if{}the{}k-point{}grid{}density{}is{}very{}low{}while{}\sigma{}is{}very{}large,{}the{}electronic{}density{}of{}states{}will{}be{}significantly{}overestimated,{}and{}numerical{}compensation{}effects{}may{}occur,{}causing{}the{}calculated{}electronic{}occupations{}to{}become{}negative.
$$

$$
因此計算超導轉變溫度時,k點網格切點密度不能過低且要進行收斂測試,尋找匹配該k點網格切點密度的\sigma,得到相對準確的超導轉變溫度.
$$

$$
Therefore,{}when{}calculating{}the{}superconducting{}transition{}temperature,{}the{}k-point{}grid{}density{}must{}not{}be{}too{}low,{}and{}convergence{}tests{}must{}be{}performed{}to{}find{}the{}value{}of{}\sigma{}that{}is{}properly{}matched{}to{}the{}k-point{}grid{}density,{}in{}order{}to{}obtain{}a{}reliable{}superconducting{}transition{}temperature.
$$
